Rhythm & Soul: Understanding Music's Role in My Life

A journey through music's influence.

Music has always been more than just background noise; it's the invisible thread weaving through the tapestry of my life. Each significant moment, from the quiet reflections of adolescence to the vibrant adventures of adulthood, is tied to a specific melody or lyric. This page is a personal journey, an attempt to visualize how the songs I’ve listened to have shaped my perspective and marked the emotional chapters of my story, especially now that I'm aware of my autism. Through data and art, I aim to show that the playlist of my life isn't just a collection of songs, but a living, breathing record of who I am.

First, more than 1,000 songs that shaped my days

This playlist is a collection of songs that have woven themselves into the fabric of my life, each one a unique trigger for memories and moments from my journey on this planet. Take "Por Siempre" by DLD, for instance; it instantly transports me back to one of the most memorable field trips I've ever shared with my close friend, Laura Olguin. Then there are the nostalgic beats of "It's My Life" by Dr. Alban and "Be My Lover" by La Bouche, which conjure images of cool, breezy nights on the beach during my secondary school days, surrounded by friends and good times. My musical tastes span generations, reaching far back before my time. While I'm a huge fan of The Beatles, my appreciation extends to classics like Édith Piaf's "La Vie en Rose" (1947) and Carlos Gardel's "Por una Cabeza" (1938). Music has also deepened my appreciation for the sheer beauty and complexity of language. While some lyrics may not carry profound personal meaning for me, the interplay of voice and instruments often creates an undeniable harmony. For example, many Black Metal songs delve into themes of evil, and while I'm not religious, I find their lyrics profound and eloquent, even if their specific meaning doesn't always resonate with my own thoughts. Interestingly, this playlist reveals a strong influence from artists and bands from the USA and UK, often overshadowing those from Mexico or even songs sung in Spanish. Given the depth and breadth of this collection, it's impossible for me to pick a single favorite song of all time. I invite you to explore it, especially those tracks you might not recognize. Perhaps some will spark a special memory for you, or connect you to a moment in my life. Or perhaps, they'll simply be a pleasure to listen to. You certainly won't have 75 hours to listen to them all at once, but I hope you find some free time to delve into a few. In the meantime, I'll be using plots to share some specific insights into my preferences within this musical journey. Yes, I know it's a bit nerdy, but that's just me – and you probably already knew that!
